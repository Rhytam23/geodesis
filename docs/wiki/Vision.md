# Vision & Core Philosophy

## The Problem
Climate planning is historically constrained by static, 300-page PDF reports that become outdated as soon as they are published. Agricultural planners, municipal governments, and farmers lack a real-time, interactive canvas where space and time act as active variables.

## The Solution
**Geodesis** replaces static reports with a map-first, interactive digital twin. By integrating satellite telemetry, weather feeds, soil properties, and market indicators, users can click any coordinate in the Mekong Delta, travel across years (2026–2050), adjust future climate scenarios, and observe physical and economic impacts in real time.

## Core Design Principles
1. **Map-First Spatial Telemetry**: Interactive geography is the primary input navigation mechanism.
2. **Transparent Explainable AI**: Every recommendation presents its formula, confidence score, and raw data sources.
3. **Resilient Adapter Layer**: If external APIs fail or are slow, deterministic browser-side fallbacks maintain uninterrupted user experience.
4. **Role-Driven Dashboards**: Tailored views for farmers, co-operatives, government officials, and researchers.
